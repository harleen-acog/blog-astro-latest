# Designing for Real Users, Not Just Aesthetics

Design isn’t just about making things look good—it’s about making them work well for real people.

## Start with user needs

Before designing:
- Who is the user?
- What problem are they solving?
- What frustrates them?

Understanding this leads to better decisions.

## Simplicity wins

Avoid clutter. Focus on:
- Clear hierarchy
- Readable typography
- Meaningful spacing

## Test early and often

Even small usability tests can reveal:
- Confusion points
- Missed expectations
- Friction in flows

## Final thoughts

Great design feels invisible. Users shouldn’t notice the design—they should just feel that it works.
