## Introduction

Artificial Intelligence is no longer just about automation.  
It’s increasingly being used to solve real scientific problems from drug discovery to climate modeling.

```js
console.log("hi")
```

For example, models like DeepMind’s AlphaFold have revolutionized how scientists understand proteins.

## The Shift in Thinking

What’s interesting is not just speed — it’s *how* problems are approached.

Instead of:
> “Let’s test this idea”

We’re moving toward:
> “Let’s explore what the data suggests”

This changes the role of scientists from experimenters to **interpreters and decision-makers**.

## Challenges

Of course, it’s not perfect:

- AI models can be **black boxes**
- Results need **human validation**
- Bias in data can lead to incorrect conclusions

## Conclusion

AI isn’t replacing science — it’s augmenting it.

The future likely belongs to a collaboration:
> Humans asking the right questions  
> AI helping find the answers
